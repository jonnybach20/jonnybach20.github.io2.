Planning Research and Monitoring Information

I took interest in the planned environment in my Masters degree, as such, my Master's dissertation was an analysis of Welsh afforestation policy's ability to satisfy current government targets. I believe the research skills I gained in my studies provided me the foundation to develop my planning research skills in my current role.

I have supported the preparation of the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park Authority's strategic plans by producing quality planning research. Here are some examples of documents I have put together for my Strategic Policy Manager which have been used to inform draft Supplementary Planning Guidance, such as SPGs for Replacement Dwellings, the retrofit of energy generation items, and robust future life of dwellings.

Furthermore, as a County with high percentage areas of second homes and holiday lets, my Manager has been considering the inclusion of a principal residency policy in the next Local Development Plan.

Regarding monitoring information, the PCNPA presents an annual monitoring report to the Welsh Government to monitor the National Park's performance and development. As Technical Support Officer, I was responsible for organising the public consultation of the Annual Monitoring Report, organising representations into a report for Officers to comment on. Once edits were agreed at commitee, I formatted the edits to produce the final Annual Monitoring Report. I was also involved in the monitoring activity, undertaking onsite survey work to monitor changes in the community in respect to retail and community facilities.
